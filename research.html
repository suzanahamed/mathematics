Research and Publications

My research interests fall broadly on the ordinary and partial differential equations; specifically, I am interested in studying nonlinear elliptic equations on bounded and unbounded domains in n-dimensional real space. Additionally, I have experience in numerical analysis, and I am interested in developing numerical methods for solving differential equations, including initial value problems with singularities at the initial points. I am currently focused on existence results and qualitative properties of solutions to the (p,q)-Laplacian equation. I am in the process of extending my work to the (p, q)-Laplacian problem \begin{equation} \Delta_p u +\Delta_q u + K(|x|)f(u)=0 \ \textrm{ on } {\mathbb R}^N\backslash \Omega\label{pq1} \end{equation} \begin{equation} u = 0 \textrm{ on } \partial \Omega, \ u\to 0 \textrm{ as } |x| \to \infty \label{pq2} \end{equation} when \( p > q > 1 \), where \(\Delta_r u=\nabla \cdot (|\nabla u|^{r-2}\nabla u)\).
In recent years, these equations have gained significant attention for their ability to model complex physical phenomena in non-Newtonian fluid mechanics, image processing, population dynamics, etc. They combine the effects of two nonlinear diffusion operators with different exponents, which generalizes the classical p-Laplacian and introduces new analytical challenges, such as the absence of homogeneity and uniform ellipticity. My current research extends the techniques we developed for p-Laplacian problems to the (p, q)-Laplacian setting. I will investigate these equations on the exterior of the open, bounded ball \(B_{R}\) in \(\mathbb{R}^N\) and try to obtain similar and/or new results.
